Warning Signs Your Engine Needs Attention

Don't ignore these critical indicators of engine problems

Check Engine Light

The most obvious sign something is wrong. Never ignore this warning light, as it could indicate various engine issues from minor to severe.

Unusual Noises

Knocking, pinging, or rattling sounds can indicate worn engine bearings, damaged pistons, or other internal engine problems.

Fluid Leaks

Oil or coolant leaks under your vehicle can indicate damaged gaskets, seals, or more serious engine problems.

Excessive Smoke

Different colored exhaust smoke can indicate different problems: blue (oil burning), white (coolant leak), or black (excess fuel).

Decreased Fuel Economy

If you're visiting the gas station more frequently, your engine may not be operating efficiently.

Loss of Power

Difficulty accelerating, climbing hills, or maintaining speed can indicate engine compression issues or fuel system problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about engine repair and maintenance

How do I know if my engine needs repair?

Common signs include the check engine light illuminating, unusual noises (knocking, grinding, or popping), decreased performance, excessive exhaust smoke, overheating, stalling, or reduced fuel efficiency. If you notice any of these symptoms, it's best to have your vehicle inspected by our professionals.

How often should I get my engine serviced?

Regular engine maintenance should follow your vehicle manufacturer's recommended service intervals, typically every 5,000 to 7,500 miles for oil changes and every 30,000 miles for more comprehensive service. However, this can vary based on your driving habits, vehicle age, and conditions.

What's the difference between engine repair and engine replacement?

Engine repair addresses specific issues with your existing engine, such as replacing gaskets, fixing oil leaks, or repairing individual components. Engine replacement involves installing an entirely new or remanufactured engine when the existing one is beyond economical repair. Our technicians will recommend the most cost-effective option based on your vehicle's condition and value.

How long does engine repair typically take?

The time required for engine repair varies greatly depending on the issue. Minor repairs might take just a few hours, while major repairs could require several days. During your initial diagnosis, our technicians will provide you with an estimated completion time for your specific repair needs.

Do you offer warranty on engine repairs?

Yes, E.M.A Tire & Auto provides warranties on all engine repairs. The specific warranty terms depend on the type of repair performed and parts used. We'll explain your warranty coverage before beginning any work on your vehicle.
