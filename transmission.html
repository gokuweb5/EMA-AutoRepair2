Warning Signs of Transmission Problems

Recognize these symptoms to prevent more serious issues

Slipping Gears

If your transmission slips in and out of gear without warning, or changes gears randomly, this indicates a serious transmission issue.

Delayed Engagement

A noticeable delay when shifting from park to drive or reverse, or a pause before the car actually engages and moves, suggests transmission problems.

Unusual Noises

Grinding, whining, or buzzing noises when the transmission shifts gears can indicate worn parts or low transmission fluid.

Rough Shifts

If your vehicle jerks, shakes, or shudders when shifting gears, your transmission may need service.

Fluid Leaks

Transmission fluid is typically red or brown. If you notice this fluid under your vehicle, you may have a transmission leak that needs immediate attention.

Warning Light

An illuminated check engine light or transmission warning light on your dashboard can indicate transmission issues that need professional diagnosis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about transmission services

How often should I change my transmission fluid?

For most vehicles, transmission fluid should be changed every 30,000 to 60,000 miles. However, this can vary based on your vehicle make and model, driving habits, and whether you have an automatic or manual transmission. Always refer to your owner's manual for specific recommendations, or ask our technicians for personalized advice.

What's the difference between a transmission flush and a fluid change?

A transmission fluid change typically replaces about 40-50% of the fluid by draining what's in the pan and adding new fluid. A transmission flush uses specialized equipment to force out all the old fluid and replace it with new fluid, providing a more complete fluid replacement. Our technicians can recommend which service is best for your vehicle based on its condition and maintenance history.

How long does a transmission typically last?

With proper maintenance, a transmission can last 150,000 to 200,000 miles or more. However, this can vary significantly based on the vehicle make and model, driving conditions, maintenance history, and whether it's an automatic or manual transmission. Regular maintenance, including fluid changes, can significantly extend the life of your transmission.

Is it better to repair or replace a failing transmission?

This depends on several factors, including the extent of the damage, the age and value of your vehicle, and the cost of the repair versus replacement. In some cases, a simple repair or rebuild may be sufficient. In others, a full replacement might be more cost-effective in the long run. Our technicians will provide a detailed assessment and honest recommendations based on your specific situation.

Can I drive with a transmission problem?

It's generally not recommended to drive with a known transmission problem, as this can lead to more extensive damage and potentially dangerous driving conditions. If you notice any warning signs of transmission issues, it's best to have your vehicle inspected as soon as possible. In some cases, continuing to drive with a transmission problem can lead to complete transmission failure, which is much more expensive to repair.
