Fuel Efficiency Tips

Maximize your vehicle's fuel economy and save money at the pump

Why Fuel Efficiency Matters

Improving your vehicle's fuel efficiency not only saves you money at the gas pump but also reduces your environmental impact and extends the life of your vehicle. With rising fuel costs, getting the most out of every gallon has never been more important.

At E.M.A Tire & Auto, we believe that small changes in driving habits and regular maintenance can make a significant difference in your fuel economy. Our expert technicians have compiled these essential tips to help you maximize your vehicle's efficiency.

Driving Habits for Better Fuel Economy

Simple changes to your driving style that can improve fuel efficiency

Efficient Driving Techniques

Driving Habits That Save Fuel

  • Maintain steady speeds: Use cruise control on highways to maintain a consistent speed.
  • Avoid rapid acceleration and braking: Gentle acceleration and anticipating stops can improve fuel economy by up to 30%.
  • Observe the speed limit: Fuel economy typically decreases rapidly at speeds above 50 mph. Each 5 mph over 50 mph is like paying an additional $0.20 per gallon for gas.
  • Reduce idling: Turn off your engine when parked for more than 30 seconds. Modern engines use less fuel starting up than idling for extended periods.
  • Plan and combine trips: Several short trips from a cold start can use twice as much fuel as one longer trip covering the same distance.
  • Use air conditioning wisely: At lower speeds, open windows have less impact on fuel economy than using A/C. At highway speeds, using A/C is typically more efficient than open windows.
  • Remove excess weight: Every 100 pounds of unnecessary weight in your vehicle reduces fuel economy by about 1%.
  • Reduce drag: Remove roof racks, cargo boxes, and bike racks when not in use to improve aerodynamics.

Maintenance for Maximum Fuel Efficiency

Keep your vehicle in top condition to optimize fuel economy

Essential Maintenance for Fuel Economy

Maintenance Tasks That Improve Fuel Efficiency

  • Keep tires properly inflated: Underinflated tires can lower gas mileage by about 0.2% for every 1 PSI drop. Check tire pressure at least monthly.
  • Use the recommended grade of motor oil: Using the manufacturer's recommended grade can improve fuel economy by 1-2%.
  • Replace air filters regularly: A clean air filter can improve acceleration time and performance, especially in older vehicles.
  • Keep the engine tuned: Fixing a serious maintenance issue, such as a faulty oxygen sensor, can improve mileage by up to 40%.
  • Check and replace spark plugs: Worn spark plugs can decrease fuel efficiency by up to 30%.
  • Inspect the fuel system: Clogged fuel injectors and dirty fuel filters can significantly reduce fuel economy.
  • Maintain wheel alignment: Misaligned wheels create drag and reduce fuel efficiency.
  • Consider fuel system cleaning: Professional fuel system cleaning can remove deposits that affect performance and fuel economy.

Warning Signs of Poor Fuel Efficiency

  • Sudden decrease in miles per gallon
  • Engine running rough or stalling
  • Difficulty starting the vehicle
  • Check engine light illuminated
  • Reduced power during acceleration

Fuel Quality and Selection

Choosing the Right Fuel for Your Vehicle

  • Use the recommended octane: Using higher octane fuel than recommended provides no benefit and wastes money.
  • Consider top-tier gasoline: These contain more detergent additives that can keep your engine cleaner.
  • Fill up during cooler times: Gasoline is denser when it's cool, so you get more fuel for your money when filling up in the morning or evening.
  • Don't overfill: Stop at the first click of the pump to avoid spills and evaporation.
  • Check your gas cap: A damaged or loose gas cap allows fuel to evaporate.
  • Consider fuel additives: Quality fuel system cleaners can help maintain fuel efficiency, especially in older vehicles.
