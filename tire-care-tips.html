Why Tire Care Matters

Your tires are the only point of contact between your vehicle and the road. Proper tire maintenance is essential not only for extending tire life and saving money but also for ensuring your safety on the road. Well-maintained tires provide better traction, handling, and braking performance in all weather conditions.

At E.M.A Tire & Auto, we believe that regular tire care is one of the most important aspects of vehicle maintenance. Our expert technicians have compiled these essential tips to help you get the most out of your tires.

Tire Pressure Maintenance

Keep your tires properly inflated for safety, performance, and longevity

Proper Inflation

Why Tire Pressure Matters

  • Safety: Properly inflated tires are less likely to fail, reducing the risk of blowouts and accidents.
  • Fuel efficiency: Underinflated tires can lower gas mileage by about 0.2% for every 1 PSI drop.
  • Tire life: Proper inflation can extend tire life by up to 20%.
  • Handling and braking: Correct tire pressure ensures optimal contact with the road for better handling and shorter stopping distances.

Tire Pressure Maintenance Tips

  • Check pressure monthly: Use a quality tire pressure gauge to check all tires, including the spare, at least once a month.
  • Check when tires are cold: Measure pressure before driving or at least three hours after driving.
  • Follow manufacturer recommendations: The correct pressure is listed in your vehicle's owner manual, on the driver's side door jamb, or inside the fuel door.
  • Don't rely on visual inspection: A tire can lose up to half its air pressure without appearing flat.
  • Adjust for temperature changes: Tire pressure can drop about 1 PSI for every 10°F decrease in temperature.
  • Don't forget the spare: Check your spare tire's pressure regularly so it's ready when needed.

Rotation, Alignment & Balancing

Essential maintenance procedures for even tire wear and optimal performance

Tire Rotation

Why Rotate Your Tires

  • Even wear: Front and rear tires wear differently due to their different roles in steering, braking, and carrying weight.
  • Extended tire life: Regular rotation can extend tire life by up to 8,000 miles.
  • Improved performance: Evenly worn tires provide better handling and traction.
  • Early problem detection: Rotation provides an opportunity to inspect tires for damage or unusual wear patterns.

Rotation Schedule and Patterns

  • Frequency: Rotate tires every 5,000-8,000 miles or according to your vehicle manufacturer's recommendation.
  • Pattern: The rotation pattern depends on whether you have front-wheel, rear-wheel, all-wheel drive, or directional tires.
  • Professional service: While rotation can be done at home with proper equipment, professional service ensures correct patterns and torque specifications.

Wheel Alignment

Signs You Need an Alignment

  • Vehicle pulling to one side while driving on a straight, level road.
  • Uneven or rapid tire wear on the edges of your tires.
  • Off-center steering wheel when driving straight.
  • Steering wheel vibration or looseness.
  • Squealing tires when making turns or driving straight.

Alignment Maintenance Tips

  • Check alignment annually: Even without symptoms, have your alignment checked once a year.
  • After hitting obstacles: Have alignment checked after hitting potholes, curbs, or other obstacles.
  • With new tires: Always check alignment when installing new tires.
  • After suspension work: Alignment should be checked after any suspension repairs or modifications.

Wheel Balancing

Signs of Unbalanced Wheels

  • Vibration in the steering wheel, floorboard, or seat at certain speeds.
  • Uneven tire wear in a scalloped or cupped pattern.
  • Increased stress on suspension components leading to premature wear.
  • Reduced fuel efficiency due to increased rolling resistance.

Balancing Maintenance Tips

  • Balance with rotation: Have your wheels balanced when rotating your tires.
  • After tire repair: Rebalance wheels after any tire repair or patch.
  • When installing new tires: Always balance wheels when installing new tires.
  • After hitting obstacles: Consider rebalancing after hitting significant potholes or curbs.

Tire Inspection & Maintenance

Regular checks to ensure tire safety and performance

Regular Tire Inspections

What to Look For During Tire Inspections

  • Tread depth: Use the penny test (insert a penny with Lincoln's head upside down into the tread; if you can see all of Lincoln's head, it's time for new tires).
  • Wear patterns: Uneven wear may indicate alignment, inflation, or suspension issues.
  • Sidewall damage: Look for cuts, cracks, bulges, or blisters that could lead to blowouts.
  • Foreign objects: Check for nails, screws, glass, or other objects embedded in the tread.
  • Valve stems: Ensure they're not cracked or damaged and have caps in place.
  • Age: Most manufacturers recommend replacing tires every 6-10 years regardless of tread depth.

Inspection Schedule

  • Monthly: Visual inspection and pressure check.
  • Before long trips: Complete inspection before traveling long distances.
  • After hitting obstacles: Check for damage after hitting potholes, curbs, or debris.
  • Seasonal: More thorough inspection when transitioning between seasons.
