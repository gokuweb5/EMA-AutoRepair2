Preventative Maintenance Tips

Regular checks and maintenance to prevent breakdowns and extend your vehicle's life

Schedule Your Maintenance Service

Why Preventative Maintenance Matters

Regular preventative maintenance is the key to extending your vehicle's life and avoiding costly repairs. By addressing small issues before they become major problems, you can save time, money, and the inconvenience of unexpected breakdowns.

At E.M.A Tire & Auto, we believe that prevention is always better than cure. Our expert technicians have compiled these essential tips to help you maintain your vehicle in optimal condition year-round.

Fluid Maintenance Tips

Keep your vehicle's vital fluids at optimal levels for peak performance

Essential Fluid Checks

Regular Fluid Maintenance Schedule

  • Engine Oil: Check monthly and change according to your vehicle's maintenance schedule (typically every 3,000-7,500 miles).
  • Coolant/Antifreeze: Check levels monthly and flush/replace according to manufacturer recommendations (typically every 30,000-50,000 miles).
  • Transmission Fluid: Check monthly and change according to your vehicle's maintenance schedule (typically every 30,000-60,000 miles).
  • Brake Fluid: Check monthly and replace every 2 years or as recommended by your vehicle manufacturer.
  • Power Steering Fluid: Check monthly and replace as needed or recommended by your vehicle manufacturer.
  • Windshield Washer Fluid: Check monthly and refill as needed with the appropriate seasonal formula.

Signs Your Fluids Need Attention

  • Dark or dirty engine oil indicates it's time for an oil change.
  • Low coolant levels or rusty coolant could lead to overheating and engine damage.
  • Dark or burnt-smelling transmission fluid may indicate internal transmission problems.
  • Dark brake fluid can indicate contamination and reduced braking efficiency.
  • Difficulty steering may indicate low power steering fluid.

Battery Care Tips

Maintain your battery for reliable starts and optimal electrical system performance

Battery Maintenance

Extending Your Battery Life

  • Regular inspections: Check for corrosion on terminals and cables. Clean with a mixture of baking soda and water if necessary.
  • Secure mounting: Ensure your battery is securely mounted to prevent vibration damage.
  • Test battery strength: Have your battery tested before extreme weather seasons (summer and winter).
  • Limit short trips: Short trips don't allow your battery to fully recharge. Take a longer drive occasionally.
  • Turn off electronics: Turn off lights, radio, and A/C before shutting off your engine to reduce the initial load on the battery at next start.
  • Replace when needed: Most batteries last 3-5 years. Consider replacement at the 3-year mark if you live in extreme climates.

Warning Signs of Battery Problems

  • Slow engine crank: When starting your vehicle, the engine turns over slowly.
  • Check engine light: A consistently illuminated check engine light may indicate battery issues.
  • Swollen battery case: Extreme heat can cause the battery case to swell.
  • Electrical issues: Dim headlights, power windows operating slowly, or radio/dashboard issues.
  • Rotten egg smell: A battery that's leaking may emit a sulfur (rotten egg) odor.

Filter Maintenance Tips

Keep your vehicle's filters clean for optimal performance and efficiency

Essential Filter Maintenance

Regular Filter Replacement Schedule

  • Air Filter: Replace every 15,000-30,000 miles or as recommended by your vehicle manufacturer. More frequently if you drive in dusty conditions.
  • Oil Filter: Replace with every oil change (typically every 3,000-7,500 miles).
  • Fuel Filter: Replace every 30,000-50,000 miles or as recommended by your vehicle manufacturer.
  • Cabin Air Filter: Replace every 15,000-25,000 miles or once a year to maintain air quality inside your vehicle.

Benefits of Clean Filters

  • Improved fuel economy: A clean air filter can improve gas mileage by up to 10%.
  • Better engine performance: Clean filters allow your engine to "breathe" properly.
  • Reduced emissions: Properly functioning filters help your vehicle run cleaner.
  • Extended engine life: Clean filters prevent contaminants from damaging engine components.
  • Improved cabin air quality: A clean cabin air filter removes allergens and pollutants from the air you breathe inside your vehicle.
