Battery Services & Replacement

Keep your vehicle starting reliably in all conditions

At E.M.A Tire & Auto, we provide comprehensive battery services to ensure your vehicle starts reliably every time. Your car's battery is essential for starting the engine and powering electrical components when the engine is off. A failing battery can leave you stranded without warning.

Our certified technicians use advanced diagnostic equipment to test your battery's condition, checking its charge level, cold cranking amps, and overall health. We offer high-quality replacement batteries for all vehicle makes and models, with professional installation and proper disposal of your old battery.

Regular battery testing is recommended as part of your routine maintenance, especially before extreme weather seasons. Most batteries last 3-5 years, but factors like climate, driving habits, and electrical demands can affect their lifespan.

Battery Types We Offer

Quality batteries for every vehicle and budget

Standard Flooded Batteries

Traditional lead-acid batteries that provide reliable performance at an economical price. Ideal for most standard vehicles with normal electrical demands.

AGM Batteries

Absorbent Glass Mat batteries offer superior performance, longer life, and better resistance to vibration. Perfect for vehicles with high electrical demands or start-stop technology.

Deep Cycle Batteries

Designed for applications requiring sustained power over longer periods. Ideal for RVs, boats, and vehicles with extensive electrical accessories.

When to Replace Your Battery

Warning signs that indicate your battery may be failing

  • Slow Engine Crank: When starting your vehicle, the engine turns over slowly or takes longer than usual to start.
  • Check Engine or Battery Warning Light: Dashboard warning lights can indicate a problem with your battery or charging system.
  • Swollen Battery Case: Extreme heat can cause the battery case to swell, indicating internal damage.
  • Battery Age: Most batteries last 3-5 years. If yours is approaching this age, consider proactive replacement.
  • Electrical Issues: Dim headlights, power windows operating slowly, or other electrical components not functioning properly.
  • Corrosion: Visible corrosion on battery terminals can indicate a leak or other issues.
  • Previous Jump Starts: If you've needed jump starts recently, your battery may be failing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about car batteries

How long do car batteries typically last?

Most car batteries last between 3-5 years, but this can vary based on climate, driving habits, and the electrical demands of your vehicle. In hot climates, batteries may fail sooner due to increased heat stress.

What causes a car battery to drain quickly?

Several factors can cause premature battery drain, including leaving lights or electronics on when the engine is off, short trips that don't allow the battery to fully recharge, extreme temperatures, corroded battery terminals, or a faulty charging system.

How often should I have my battery tested?

We recommend having your battery tested at least twice a year, especially before extreme weather seasons. If your battery is over 3 years old, more frequent testing may be beneficial to catch potential issues early.

Do you recycle old batteries?

Yes, we properly recycle all old batteries according to environmental regulations. Car batteries contain lead and acid that can be harmful to the environment if not disposed of correctly. Our recycling program ensures these materials are processed safely.
