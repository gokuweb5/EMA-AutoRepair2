Expert Fuel System Services

Keep your engine running smoothly with professional fuel system maintenance

Your vehicle's fuel system is responsible for delivering the right amount of fuel to your engine at the precise time needed for optimal performance. Over time, fuel injectors, fuel pumps, and fuel filters can become clogged with deposits and debris, leading to decreased performance and fuel efficiency.

At E.M.A Tire & Auto, our certified technicians provide comprehensive fuel system services to keep your vehicle running at its best. From fuel injector cleaning to fuel pump replacement, we have the expertise and equipment to handle all your fuel system needs.

Warning Signs of Fuel System Problems

Recognize these symptoms to prevent more serious issues

Decreased Performance

Sluggish acceleration, hesitation when pressing the gas pedal, or overall reduced power can indicate fuel system issues.

Poor Fuel Economy

If you're getting fewer miles per gallon than usual, your fuel system may not be operating efficiently.

Engine Misfires

Sputtering, jerking, or hesitation during acceleration can be signs of fuel delivery problems.

Check Engine Light

An illuminated check engine light can indicate various issues, including problems with your fuel system components.

Hard Starting

Difficulty starting your vehicle, especially after it sits for a while, can be related to fuel pump or fuel pressure issues.

Unusual Odors

The smell of gasoline around your vehicle can indicate a fuel leak, which requires immediate attention for safety reasons.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about fuel system services

How often should I have my fuel system serviced?

We recommend having your fuel system inspected and serviced every 30,000 miles or as recommended in your vehicle's owner manual. However, if you notice any symptoms of fuel system problems, it's best to have it checked right away.

What are the benefits of fuel injector cleaning?

Fuel injector cleaning can improve fuel economy, restore lost power, reduce emissions, improve engine performance, and extend the life of your fuel system components. It's a cost-effective way to maintain your vehicle's performance and efficiency.

How long does a fuel filter typically last?

Fuel filters typically last between 30,000 and 50,000 miles, but this can vary depending on your vehicle make and model, driving conditions, and fuel quality. Some newer vehicles have lifetime fuel filters that are designed to last the life of the vehicle, but they may still require service or replacement in certain conditions.

What causes fuel injectors to become clogged?

Fuel injectors can become clogged due to carbon deposits from fuel, dirt and debris in the fuel system, poor quality fuel, and normal wear and tear. Regular fuel system maintenance and using quality fuel can help prevent injector clogging.

Can I use fuel additives instead of professional fuel system cleaning?

While fuel additives can help maintain your fuel system between professional services, they typically don't provide the same level of cleaning as professional equipment. Our fuel system service uses specialized equipment that can apply higher pressure and more effective cleaning solutions than what's available in consumer additives. For best results, we recommend a combination of regular use of quality fuel additives and periodic professional fuel system cleaning.
