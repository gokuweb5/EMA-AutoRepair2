When Can a Tire Be Repaired?

Not all tire damage can be repaired. Here are some guidelines we follow:

  • Punctures in the tread area that are 1/4 inch or smaller
  • Damage that is at least 1/2 inch away from the sidewall
  • Sidewall damage is generally not repairable
  • Multiple punctures that are close together
  • Tires with excessive wear (less than 2/32" tread depth)

Frequently Asked Questions About Tire Repair

How long does a tire repair take?

Most standard tire repairs can be completed in 30-45 minutes. However, this may vary depending on the type and extent of the damage.

Is it safe to drive with a nail in my tire?

It's not recommended to drive with a nail in your tire as it can lead to a sudden loss of air pressure. If you must drive, do so cautiously and at reduced speeds to the nearest repair facility.

How much does tire repair cost?

Our standard tire repairs start at $25 per tire. The final cost may vary based on the type of repair needed and your specific vehicle.

Can all punctures be repaired?

No, not all punctures can be safely repaired. Factors such as the size and location of the puncture, as well as the overall condition of the tire, determine if a repair is possible.
